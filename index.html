About

Dr. Richard
A. Price

I'm Dr. Richard A. Price ‚Äî a white cis-gendered gay scholar and Assistant Professor of Special Education and Applied Behavior Analysis in the Educational Studies Department at Purdue University's College of Education.

Dr. Price has over a decade of experience supporting the transition from school to work for individuals with disabilities. This commitment was shaped by growing up with a family member with a disability and witnessing firsthand the systemic shortcomings in including people with disabilities in education and society.

Dr. Price's work sits at the intersection of vocational assessment, self-determination, adaptive sport, ethics in applied behavior analysis, and the intersection of Disability and LGBTQ+ identities. Dr. Price co-founded The Next Step Programs (TNS), a nonprofit supporting individuals with IDD transitioning to employment, independent living, and postsecondary education, and currently provides sexuality and healthy relationship education at the Wabash Center in Lafayette, IN.

Research Focus

Transition from school to work for students with significant support needs ‚Äî vocational assessments, self-determination, and career development.

Teaching Philosophy

Rigorous thinking doesn't have to be joyless. Dr. Price has been ranked as an excellent teacher by students multiple semesters running at UIUC and now at Purdue.

Identity & Advocacy

Passionate about the intersection of Disability and LGBTQ+ identities and committed to inclusive, affirming practice in education, research, and community.
